SISTINE MADONNA

(c 1512 - 1514)

Sistine Madonna


Artist: Raphael (Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino)
Location: Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister, Dresden, Germany
Created: 1512–1514
Media: Oil on canvas
Periods: High Renaissance
Dimensions: 265 cm × 196 cm (104 in × 77 in)
Genres: Religious Art, Madonna and Child, Renaissance Painting

ARTIST


Raphael (Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino) (1483 – 1520) was an Italian painter and architect, widely considered one of the greatest artists of the High Renaissance.

Raphael is renowned for his paintings, frescoes, and his contributions to architecture, particularly his work in the Vatican. During his lifetime, he enjoyed widespread fame and was highly regarded by his contemporaries. His career was marked by a prolific output of works that were praised for their clarity, harmony, and grace. Raphael was appointed the chief architect of St. Peter's Basilica in Rome shortly before his death. Though he died at a young age, his influence on Western art is immense, with his works continuing to be celebrated for their mastery of composition, color, and form.

ARTWORK INFORMATION


The Sistine Madonna is a masterpiece by Raphael, painted between 1512 and 1514. This iconic work depicts the Virgin Mary holding the Christ Child, flanked by Saint Sixtus and Saint Barbara, set against a rich, divine landscape. The composition is known for its serene beauty and grace, with a profound sense of spirituality. The figures of Mary and Christ are the focal point, radiating purity and tenderness, while the two angels at the bottom of the painting, with their playful and endearing expressions, add a sense of warmth and humanity.

Raphael’s exceptional use of color and light enhances the peaceful and ethereal mood of the painting. The soft folds of the drapery and the luminous quality of the figures convey a sense of divine presence, while the delicate details of the saints and angels invite a closer inspection of the painting's fine execution. The figures appear suspended between heaven and earth, creating a harmonious blend of the divine and earthly realms.

The Sistine Madonna is often interpreted as a symbol of maternal love and the divine nature of Christ, celebrated for its serene beauty, composition, and the emotional connection between the figures. Today, the painting is housed in the Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister in Dresden, where it continues to captivate viewers with its timeless grace and spiritual depth.
