RAPHEAL SANZIO

Full Name RAFFAELLO SANZIO DA URBINO
An Italian Renaissance artist acclaimed for his harmonious paintings like The School of Athens and the Sistine Madonna. His work embodies balance, clarity, and Renaissance ideals.

Raphael Sanzio (1483–1520) was an Italian painter and architect of the High Renaissance. Celebrated for his harmonious compositions and refined technique, his works, such as The School of Athens and his Madonna paintings, epitomize the balance and grace of the Renaissance aesthetic.


Raphael’s oeuvre includes frescoes, portraits, and religious works, celebrated for their harmony, grace, and clarity. Paintings like The School of Athens and Sistine Madonna exemplify his mastery of perspective and composition. A prolific artist, Raphael became one of the most sought-after painters in Renaissance Rome before his life was tragically cut short at 37.
