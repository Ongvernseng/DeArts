SANDRO BOTTICELLI

Full Name ALESSANDRO DI MARIANO DI VANNI FILIPEPI
An Italian Renaissance painter known for his ethereal works such as The Birth of Venus and Primavera.
His art blends mythology and beauty with graceful composition and vivid imagination.

Sandro Botticelli (1445–1510) was an Italian painter of the Early Renaissance. Famous for his poetic and graceful style, his masterpieces like The Birth of Venus and Primavera reflect the ideals of beauty and mythology that defined the Florentine Renaissance.


Botticelli’s art is known for its ethereal beauty, lyrical compositions, and focus on mythology. Works like The Birth of Venus and Primavera blend classical themes with a delicate, otherworldly style. While he experienced fame in his lifetime, Botticelli’s reputation faded after his death, only to be revived centuries later during the 19th century’s rediscovery of Renaissance art.
