Mona Lisa

(c.1503-1519)

MONA LISA


Artist: Leonardo da Vinci
Location: Louvre Museum, Paris
Created: 1503–1506 (possibly worked on until 1517)
Media: Oil on wood panel
Periods: Renaissance, High Renaissance
Dimensions: 77 cm x 53 cm
Genres: Portrait, Renaissance art

ARTIST


Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci (1452 – 1519) was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance who was active as a painter, draughtsman, engineer, scientist, theorist, sculptor, and architect.

While his fame initially rested on his achievements as a painter, he has also become known for his notebooks, in which he made drawings and notes on a variety of subjects, including anatomy, astronomy, botany, cartography, painting, and palaeontology. Leonardo is widely regarded to have been a genius who epitomised the Renaissance humanist ideal and his collective works comprise a contribution to later generations of artists matched only by that of his younger contemporary Michelangelo.

ARTWORK INFORMATION


Mona Lisa, painted by Leonardo da Vinci between 1503 and 1506, with possible refinements extending until 1517, is one of the most famous paintings in the world. The portrait is believed to depict Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a wealthy Florentine merchant, Francesco del Giocondo. The painting's name, La Gioconda, is derived from her married name. What makes the Mona Lisa so iconic is her enigmatic expression, particularly her smile, which seems to shift depending on the viewer’s perspective. This mysterious quality has intrigued art historians, critics, and audiences for centuries.

Leonardo employed the technique of sfumato, a method of blending light and shadow to create smooth transitions and soft edges. This gives the painting a lifelike quality, enhancing the illusion of depth and realism. The background features a distant landscape, creating an atmospheric effect that contrasts with the figure in the foreground. The subtle modeling of light on her face and hands adds to the sense of naturalism, a hallmark of the Renaissance period.

Today, the Mona Lisa is housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris, where it attracts millions of visitors each year. It remains one of the most studied and celebrated works of art in history, embodying the genius of Leonardo da Vinci and the Renaissance era.
