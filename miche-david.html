ARTIST


Michelangelo Buonarroti (1475 – 1564) was an Italian sculptor, painter, architect, and poet, widely considered one of the most influential figures in the history of Western art.

Known for his mastery of the human form, Michelangelo’s work spans the High Renaissance, and he is renowned for his contributions to sculpture, most famously the statue of David and the Pietà. As a painter, his frescoes on the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel, particularly The Creation of Adam, are among the greatest achievements of Renaissance art. Michelangelo's genius extended to architecture, where he played a major role in the design of St. Peter's Basilica in Vatican City. His works are characterized by their emotional intensity, exceptional skill, and profound understanding of human anatomy. Despite his fame and success, Michelangelo faced significant personal and financial challenges throughout his life.

ARTWORK INFORMATION


David is a masterpiece by Michelangelo Buonarroti, created between 1501 and 1504. This iconic sculpture depicts the biblical hero David before his battle with Goliath. Unlike earlier representations, Michelangelo's David is shown in a moment of calm and contemplation, displaying both physical strength and psychological tension. The figure stands tall and confident, with a slingshot over his shoulder, symbolizing courage and divine favor.

Michelangelo’s mastery of marble is evident in this sculpture. The figure's muscular form, the detailed rendering of David's face, and the intricate folds of his skin demonstrate the artist’s exceptional understanding of human anatomy. The pose, known as "contrapposto," creates a dynamic sense of movement, even though the figure is still. The fine details, such as the veins and the expression on David's face, further enhance the realism of the sculpture.

David is often interpreted as a symbol of human strength, faith, and the triumph of good over evil. Its monumental scale and emotional depth make it one of the most celebrated sculptures in art history. Today, the sculpture is housed in the Galleria dell'Accademia in Florence, where it continues to captivate viewers with its beauty and power.
