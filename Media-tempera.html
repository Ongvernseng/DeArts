Tempera


An ancient painting technique using pigments mixed with egg yolk,
known for its durability, vibrant finish, and quick drying, popular in medieval and Renaissance art.

Fresco


Tempera is one of the oldest painting techniques, known for its durability and vibrant finish. This medium involves mixing colored pigments with a water-soluble binder, traditionally egg yolk, which creates a quick-drying paint. Artists apply tempera onto a smooth, rigid surface, typically wood panels prepared with a gesso undercoat. The medium allows for precise detail and luminous, matte finishes but requires careful layering, as it dries rapidly and is less forgiving than oil paints. Tempera was widely used during the medieval and early Renaissance periods, with masterpieces like Botticelli’s Primavera showcasing its capability for intricate detail and vivid color.

-mostly used on panel or stone wall
