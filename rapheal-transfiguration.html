Transfiguration


Artist: Raphael (Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino)
Location: Vatican Museums, Vatican City
Created: 1516–1520
Media: Oil on panel
Periods: High Renaissance
Dimensions: 405 cm × 278 cm (159 in × 109 in)
Genres: Religious Art, Biblical Painting, History Painting

ARTIST


Raphael (Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino) (1483 – 1520) was an Italian painter and architect, widely considered one of the greatest artists of the High Renaissance.

Raphael is renowned for his paintings, frescoes, and his contributions to architecture, particularly his work in the Vatican. During his lifetime, he enjoyed widespread fame and was highly regarded by his contemporaries. His career was marked by a prolific output of works that were praised for their clarity, harmony, and grace. Raphael was appointed the chief architect of St. Peter's Basilica in Rome shortly before his death. Though he died at a young age, his influence on Western art is immense, with his works continuing to be celebrated for their mastery of composition, color, and form.

ARTWORK INFORMATION


The Transfiguration is a masterpiece by Raphael, painted between 1516 and 1520. This iconic work depicts the biblical scene of the Transfiguration of Christ, showcasing the moment when Jesus is gloriously transformed in front of his disciples on Mount Tabor. In the upper portion of the composition, Christ is shown in radiant light, flanked by Moses and Elijah, while below, the apostles struggle to heal a possessed boy. The painting captures both the divine and human aspects of the story, making it a profound depiction of faith and divine revelation.

Raphael’s brilliance in composition and color is evident in this work. The dramatic contrast between light and shadow creates a dynamic sense of movement, while the figures are rendered with exceptional detail and emotional expression. The heavenly scene above contrasts with the earthly, troubled scene below, highlighting the tension between the divine and human realms.

The Transfiguration is often interpreted as a symbol of Christ’s divinity and the triumph of good over evil. Its spiritual depth, dramatic composition, and emotional resonance make it one of Raphael’s most revered masterpieces. Today, the painting is housed in the Vatican Museums, where it continues to inspire awe and admiration for its technical brilliance and religious significance.
