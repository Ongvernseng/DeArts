LEONARDO DA VINCI

Full Name LEONARDO DI SER PIERO DA VINCI
An Italian Renaissance polymath renowned for his masterpieces like Mona Lisa and The Last Supper.
A visionary, he excelled in art, science, and invention.

Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci (1452 – 1519) was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance who was active as a painter, draughtsman, engineer, scientist, theorist, sculptor, and architect.


Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519) was a true Renaissance polymath, excelling as a painter, inventor, scientist, and engineer. His art, including masterpieces like Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, reflects his meticulous study of anatomy, perspective, and nature, blending science and creativity to achieve unparalleled realism and emotional depth. Renowned for his innovative techniques, such as sfumato and chiaroscuro, Leonardo’s works embody the intellectual and artistic spirit of the Renaissance, making him a symbol of genius and curiosity. His notebooks, filled with sketches and observations, reveal his insatiable quest for understanding the world​
